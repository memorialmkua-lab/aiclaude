# Door Closer Selection — Design Guidance & Product Comparison
**Project Type:** Primary School — Double-Glazed Aluminium Doors (~2.4m tall)
**Jurisdiction:** Victoria, Australia
**Prepared in accordance with:** NCC 2022, AS1428.1:2009, DDA 1992

---

## Part 1: Regulatory Requirements — VBA / NCC 2022 / DDA

### Mandatory Design Requirements for Accessible Doors

| Requirement | Standard / Source | Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **Maximum door opening force: ≤ 20N** | DDA 1992 / NCC 2022 Part D4 / AS1428.1:2009 | Applies to all doors on an accessible path of travel. Non-compliance is a breach of Deemed-to-Satisfy provisions. |
| **Door closer with hold-open or delayed action** | NCC 2022 Part D4 | All door closers on accessible doors must include an adjustable delayed action or hold-open function. |
| **Doorstop required** | VBA Design Guidance / NCC 2022 | A doorstop must be installed to prevent damage to walls, hardware, and glazing, and to protect users from door rebound. |
| **Closer must be EN1154 compliant** | AS1905.1 / EN1154 | Required for fire-rated doors and doors on accessible paths. |
| **Fire door performance** | AS1905.1:2005 | Door closers on fire doors must be tested and rated appropriately for the fire compartment. |

> **Key compliance note:** Under NCC 2022, the 20N maximum opening force applies when the door is fitted with a self-closing device. This is the single most critical requirement for door closer selection — and the most commonly failed item on accessible paths in schools.

## Part 3: Pros & Cons

### Carbine CDC-5 Radius — Rack & Pinion (EN2–6, 40–120kg)

**Pros**
- Lower upfront cost
- Adjustable across EN2–6 power range
- Non-handed installation
- 500,000 cycle tested (EN1154)
- Adequate for standard interior commercial doors

**Cons**
- **Rack and pinion mechanism creates high initial opening resistance** — on a heavy aluminium door at EN6, consistently achieving the mandatory ≤20N opening force is not reliably possible. This is a direct DDA compliance risk.
- **Closing force decreases toward the end of travel** — when wind load is applied to a 2.4m tall glazed door, the door may fail to latch positively. This is a safety hazard in an exposed school environment.
- **2-hour fire rating only** — may not satisfy fire compartment requirements for a school building where 4-hour-rated compartments are often specified.
- **Not AS1428.1 DDA certified** — no independent certification of compliance with Australian accessibility standards.
- **Minimum EN2 floor** — cannot be adjusted below EN2, limiting use on lighter or sensitive accessible doors.
- **Hold-open requires a separate arm accessory** — adds installation complexity and additional cost.

---

### Lockwood 2616 CAM Action (EN1–6, 20–120kg)

**Pros**
- **CAM action technology delivers a smooth, low and consistent opening force throughout the full door arc** — engineered to comply with the DDA/AS1428.1 ≤20N requirement even at EN6 for heavy doors. This is the critical differentiator.
- **Full EN1–6 range** — precisely adjustable from light interior doors right up to 120kg heavy aluminium glazed doors.
- **Dual pressure relief valves** — provides positive, controlled closing force that overcomes wind load on tall exterior aluminium doors without slamming. Essential for 2.4m exposed glazed entries.
- **4-hour fire rating (AS1905.1:2005 compliant)** — exceeds standard school fire door requirements with margin to spare.
- **AS1428.1 DDA certified** — independently tested and certified for Australian accessibility compliance. Eliminates compliance risk.
- **Built-in hold-open adjustable from 0° to 120°** — natively satisfies the NCC 2022 hold-open device requirement with no additional accessories.
- **25-year product warranty** — aligned with the asset lifecycle expectations of school infrastructure projects.
- **Cast aluminium body (high-performance silicon alloy)** — built for sustained high-traffic use in demanding public environments.
- **70–80% hydraulic efficiency** — smooth, consistent operation suitable for children, elderly, and users with disabilities.
- **Adjustable back check** — protects door, frame, and glazing from wind-driven impact. Mandatory consideration for 2.4m tall glazed doors.

**Cons**
- Higher upfront cost than the Carbine option (reflects premium performance and warranty)
- Requires accurate installation alignment for optimal performance

## Part 4: Why the Lockwood 2616 is the Right Choice

This project has three non-negotiable requirements that directly eliminate the Carbine CDC-5 as a viable option:

**1. DDA Compliance (≤20N Opening Force)**
Rack and pinion closers generate high initial opening force by mechanical design. On a double-glazed aluminium door approaching 120kg, the Carbine CDC-5 at EN6 cannot reliably meet the 20N threshold. The Lockwood 2616's CAM action profile is specifically engineered so that opening force remains low and constant throughout the swing — it is independently AS1428.1 certified. One product eliminates the compliance risk; the other introduces it.

**2. Wind Resistance on Tall Glazed Doors**
A 2.4m double-glazed aluminium door has a large surface area that acts like a sail under wind load. The Carbine's rack and pinion mechanism loses closing force in the final degrees of travel — exactly where it's needed most to overcome wind pressure and achieve a positive latch. The Lockwood 2616's dual pressure relief valves maintain controlled closing force throughout the full arc, ensuring reliable latching regardless of wind condition.

**3. Primary School Environment**
Children exert less force than adults. The Lockwood 2616's CAM action profile makes the door easier to push open and ensures it closes quietly and positively — reducing door-related injuries, improving safety, and maintaining the inclusive design intent required in a school setting. The 25-year warranty also represents significantly better long-term value for a public infrastructure asset.
